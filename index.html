Shopping Trends Overview by Location

Taking the state as the starting point, explore the geographical distribution, regional preferences and consumption patterns of shopping activities.

Key Findings

  • The distribution of customers is relatively scattered, and no state shows an absolute concentration of customers, indicating that the business covers a wide area.
  • The proportion of customers younger than 20 years old is relatively low, and the market share of young consumers is insufficient.
  • The proportion of all types of consumer behavior is average, and there is no special preference.

Shopping Trends Overview by Category

Taking the category as the starting point, explore the geographical distribution, regional preferences and consumption patterns of shopping activities.

Key Findings

  • The core of revenue is Clothing, which is far more than other categories.
  • There is a huge gap between the number of single products sold and the sales volume.

Who's Buying? A Gender and Age Structure Analysis of Consumers

Explore the distribution of customers by their gender and age.

Key Findings

  • Men are the core customer group, accounting for nearly twice the proportion of women, indicating that there is obvious gender bias in the business audience
  • The customer group is mainly middle-aged and old, while the young group (15-20 years old) has less coverage, and the age structure is more mature

Are Promotional Tactics Really Effective?

Explore the impact of discounts, regular customers, subscriptions, and delivery methods on purchase unit prices.

Key Findings

  • Discount and subscription status are two kinds of common operation means, but they do not drive the average purchase amount of customers in a single time.
  • Regardless of the number of historical purchases (loyalty), there is little difference in the average purchase amount of a single purchase among different customer groups, and the amount dimension of consumption behavior is highly stable.
  • The average purchase amount corresponding to various distribution methods is basically the same, and customers' choice of distribution methods is not related to their own consumption amount.

What Influenced Our Review Rating?

Explore what factors affect customer evaluations.

Key Findings

  • No matter the category, gender, delivery method or subscription status, the average score difference is very small, which indicates that users' evaluation of different types of products and service experiences is relatively consistent.
  • Different categories and colors have different scores in different seasons. For example, footwear in autumn and gray in winter have the highest rating.
  • Although the average score of different items fluctuated slightly, it was still concentrated in the range of 3.6-3.9. There was no extremely low score item, and the overall evaluation was at a stable level above the middle.

Do Our Customers Like Our Outerwear?

Explore the sales performance of different categories of products in different seasons.

Key Findings

  • No matter in summer, winter, spring or autumn, the purchase amount of Clothing is far higher than that of other categories, and it is the main revenue in the whole season.
  • The performance of the same category varies significantly in different seasons, reflecting the correlation between season and category demand.

What Should We Do Next?

Strategic recommendations based on comprehensive data analysis to drive growth, improve customer satisfaction, and optimize marketing efforts

Product Strategy

  • Focus on core product category resources: Clothing is the main revenue driver across all seasons, with balanced sales across individual items. Investment in SKU updates and quality optimization for this category can be increased. For weaker categories such as Outerwear, non-core styles can be reduced, and resources can be concentrated on developing best-sellers.
  • Supplement products for young customers: Currently, our customers are mainly middle-aged and elderly individuals. We can develop clothing/accessory styles that meet the preferences of the 15-20 year old demographic, filling the gap in the young market.

Marketing Strategy

  • Application scenarios for adjusting discounts: If discounts do not significantly increase the average transaction value, it is recommended to use discounts for "attracting new customers + clearing inventory" rather than increasing the average transaction value.
  • Optimizing the value proposition of subscription services: Subscription status does not affect the amount of consumption. Non-monetary benefits such as "exclusive early access to new products, point accumulation" can be included in subscription benefits to enhance the attractiveness of subscriptions.

Customer Experience

  • Weak links in stable product evaluation: Although the overall evaluation is balanced, there are still 17.56% of negative reviews. It is recommended to establish an after-sales follow-up mechanism for the links where negative reviews are concentrated (such as logistics timeliness and product quality) to quickly solve problems.
  • Simplify the decision-making cost of shipping options: Since the shipping method does not affect the total purchase amount, "Free Shipping" can be set as the default option, reducing customers' cost in choosing shipping methods and enhancing the efficiency of placing orders.
